I. Theoretical Frameworks

Explore the three lenses used by academia to analyze innovation adoption.

Technology-Organization-Environment

The TOE framework remains the gold standard for organizational studies. It posits that adoption is not just a technical choice, but a complex interaction between internal readiness and external pressure.

"A comprehensive model to explain organizational behavior regarding technological change." — Tornatzky (1990)
Technological: Relative advantage and compatibility.
Organizational: Resource availability and firm size.
Environmental: Competitive pressure and regulation.

Technology Acceptance Model

While TOE looks at the firm, TAM looks at the employee. AI research has recently added "Trust" to the original Perceived Usefulness (PU) and Perceived Ease of Use (PEOU) factors.

Key Finding: In GenAI, "Trust in Algorithm" is now 2.4x more predictive of adoption than "Ease of Use."

Diffusion of Innovations

Rogers' classic curve explains the speed of AI's spread. GenAI has seen the fastest transition from "Innovators" to "Early Majority" in history.
